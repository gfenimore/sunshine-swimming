5 minutes

Settings → Company Settings → scroll to Tags section.

+ Create tag: "qualified" → Color: Green + Create tag: "declined" → Color: Red + Create tag: "nonprofit" → Color: Purple + Create tag: "pool-issue" → Color: Orange

Tags can be applied to projects manually (from the project sidebar) or automatically via automations. In the automation workflow, we'll use "tag added" as a trigger condition to route leads into the right email sequence.

Each lesson is 30 minutes, one-on-one with your child in your pool (or the location you listed). I come to you — no need to drive anywhere! Sessions are typically once or twice per week, and most swimmers see real progress in 4–6 lessons.

🌧️ Rain Policy

Your pool should be heated to at least 85°F for little ones — cold water makes it hard to learn and isn't safe for infants and toddlers. If you're using a neighborhood or clubhouse pool, just make sure I'll have access.

I'll give you a call in the next day or two to chat and get you on the calendar. In the meantime, keep an eye out for another email from me tomorrow with some important water safety tips!

Looking forward to meeting {swimmer_name}! 💙

13

Email #2 — Water Safety Do's & Don'ts

10 minutes

Sent automatically 1 day after the welcome email. Pre-answers the questions Amanda says she gets asked constantly.

From: Amanda @ Sunshine Swimming Subject: Before our first lesson — important water safety tips 🛟

Hi {client_first_name}!

As we get ready for {swimmer_name}'s first lesson, here are some things that will make a big difference:

🚫 Please Don't Use

Life jackets, puddle jumpers, or arm floaties — I know they feel safe, but they actually teach kids the wrong body position in the water. They create a false sense of confidence and make it harder to learn real swimming skills. If your child has been using them, that's OK — we'll work through it together!

✅ Do This Before Lessons

Practice in the bathtub! Get your child comfortable with water on their face. Pour water gently over their head, practice blowing bubbles, let them splash. The more comfortable they are with water on their face, the faster lessons go.

Swim diapers are required for any child not fully potty trained. Regular diapers swell up like a balloon in the pool — not fun for anyone!

Sunscreen — apply at least 15 minutes before the lesson so it absorbs. Slippery, freshly-sunscreened kids are hard to hold onto!

👀 During Lessons

You're welcome to watch! For toddlers, sometimes having a parent nearby helps them feel safe. Sometimes it doesn't — we'll figure out what works for {swimmer_name}. If your child cries the first lesson, that's completely normal. It almost always gets better by lesson 2 or 3.

Questions? Just reply to this email or text me anytime.

— Amanda

Demo Flow — What You Say

15 minutes

OPEN (2 min):

"Amanda, remember when you told me you want a system that filters people in and out before they call you? I built it. Let me show you — this is running live right now on a free trial."

SHOW THE FORM (3 min):

Pull up the intake form. Walk through the fields. Point out the service area dropdown: "See this? If someone from St. Pete fills this out, they never reach you — the system handles it."

LIVE SUBMISSION (3 min):

Fill out the form live as "Arwen Undómiel" — select "Outside Manatee County." Submit it. Then switch to HoneyBook and show:

  • The new project appeared in the pipeline
  • Show how you'd tag it "declined" and the referral email fires
  • Switch to your email — show the referral email that arrived

SHOW THE PIPELINE (3 min):

Show the CRM with the test records already loaded: "Look — here are your qualified leads. Frodo, Sam, Pippin, Merry. Each one already received the welcome email, the water safety tips, and the booking nudge — automatically. You didn't touch a thing."

SHOW AN EMAIL (2 min):

Open one of the welcome emails that was sent. "This is your content — your policies, your FAQ, your voice. It just sends itself now."

SHOW THE SCHEDULER (2 min):

Show the scheduling page, the 30-min slots, the availability. Mention SMS reminders: "Every client gets a text 24 hours before and 1 hour before. No more no-shows, no more 'I forgot.'"

CLOSE:

"This is a working prototype — not a presentation. If you like what you see, I'll build the final version on YOUR HoneyBook account with your real branding, your referral list, and your actual schedule. And we'll do it together so you own it completely."
